How I lost 11kg in 4 months.

A complete first-principles and scientific breakdown of how to lose weight.

Where I started

For ten years I thought I could out-train a bad diet.

I was in the gym the whole time. That was never the problem.

I ate fried snacks and dessert almost every day and told myself the workout would burn it off. It never did. Five real attempts over a decade, all the same ending: heavier than I wanted, no energy, half-convinced this was just my body.

The fifth time I stopped collecting tips and learned how the body actually works. It is simpler than the fitness industry wants you to think. Start with the question nobody answers.

First principle · where fat goes
When you lose fat, where does it actually go?

Most people guess sweat. Or that it "turns into energy" and vanishes. Both wrong.

You breathe it out.

Fat and carbs are just chains of carbon atoms. When your body breaks them apart for energy, that carbon has to leave. It grabs the oxygen you breathe in and walks out as carbon dioxide, every single time you exhale. Galpin puts it simply: fat loss is carbon in versus carbon out.

Roughly 84% of the fat you lose leaves through your lungs. The rest as water.

This sounds like trivia. It is the most useful thing I learned. It means there is one real rule for fat loss: put in less carbon than you breathe out. That is a calorie deficit, said in atoms.

And it kills the lie I lived in for a decade. Burning fat in a workout is not the same as losing fat. You can do a fasted run that burns mostly fat and lose nothing, because if you eat it all back, the carbon never leaves. The workout helps. It does not excuse the plate.

The one law

Eat less than you burn. Everything else is detail.

Every diet that works, works because it gets you into a deficit. Keto, fasting, counting, none of them beat this law, they just trick you into following it. The hard part is how fast you can undo it. Try the numbers.

Tool · can you out-train it?
Burning calories is slow. Eating them back takes four minutes. That gap is why the plate wins.
The plate · the food that won it

I added cardio for ten years. The fix was on my plate.

Two things make protein the one food worth obsessing over.

It is expensive to digest. Your body burns 20 to 30% of protein's calories just breaking it down. For fried snacks it is almost nothing. So 100 calories of eggs cost you less than 100 calories of chips, before you have done anything.

Calories your body burns just digesting
Protein20–30%
Carbs5–10%
Fat0–3%

It is the only food that turns hunger off. Your body keeps you hungry until it gets enough protein. Eat low-protein meals and you keep eating, chips, dessert, more of everything, chasing a target you never reach. Scientists call it the protein leverage effect. I call it the reason my cravings disappeared. I stopped fighting them. I was just full.

The number that works is about 1.6g of protein per kg of bodyweight, more if you train hard. At 90kg that is ~144g. I ran 120 to 150g a day from whey, eggs, tofu and paneer, with carbs and fat kept in balance, not cut.

If you're a beginner, read this

Do not try to guess your calories alone. For the first 14 days I worked with a nutritionist who set my numbers and cut the junk. I sent a photo of every meal to a WhatsApp group. No cheat days, not even Sunday.

The first week was hard. By day seven I had adapted. By day 14 it felt normal. After that there was no diet to "stay on." I knew what the right plate looked like, so it was just repetition. If you understand the science, or you hand your diet to Claude, you can skip the guesswork yourself.

The engine room · how you make energy

You run on three engines. They switch on in order.

You do not need a biology degree. But once you see how your body makes energy, training finally makes sense.

The three energy systems
0–10 sec
1 ATP
The instant engine
Stored in the muscle. A heavy lift or short sprint. Fires now, gone in seconds. Like a matchstick.
10s–2 min
~4 ATP
The sugar engine
Burns glucose fast for hard efforts. Makes acid, that's the burn at the end of a sprint.
2 min +
~32 ATP
The oxygen engine
In your mitochondria, with oxygen, burns sugar and fat. Makes ~10x more energy. This is the fat-burning one.
The lactate myth: lactate is not the villain behind the burn or next-day soreness. It is fuel. Your heart, brain and liver recycle it for energy. It is the cleanup crew, not the enemy.

The skill that matters: switching fuel

A healthy body switches between fat and sugar without you noticing. At rest you run mostly on fat. Flat out, you run almost entirely on sugar. Drag the intensity and watch the mix change.

Training · name what you train

"Get fit" is useless. Galpin made it a list.

This is what hit me hardest. Fitness is not one thing. Even endurance is four different things, each trained differently. You cannot improve what you cannot name. So I named them, and trained each on purpose.

💪

Muscular endurance

How long one muscle lasts. Planks, push-ups.
LimitHow fast the muscle clears waste, and its tiny blood vessels.
TrainHigh reps near failure with light loads. Grows new capillaries.
🔥

Anaerobic capacity

All-out for 20 sec to 2 min. One brutal hill sprint.
LimitHow fast your body buffers acid.
TrainShort max sprints, long rests. 20s on, 40 to 60s off.
❤️

VO₂ max

Your aerobic ceiling. 5 to 15 min flat out, a hard mile.
LimitHow much blood your heart pumps per beat.
TrainMile repeats, 1 to 2x a week. Lowers your resting heart rate.
🏃

Long-duration (Zone 2)

30 min to hours, easy pace, can still talk.
LimitBreathing and posture, not your legs.
TrainSteady 30 to 120 min. Galpin's tip: breathe through your nose only.
Source: Dr. Andy Galpin on Huberman Lab, "How to Build Endurance & Lose Fat." The whole week takes about two hours.

Body fat went from 27% to 19%. About 84% of what I lost was fat, not muscle. Crash diets burn muscle, which drops your metabolism and brings the weight back. Lifting in a deficit tells your body to keep the muscle and pull from fat instead.

Then I gave it a number

"Get fit" is a mood. A target weight, a weekly weigh-in, and fixed training days is a system. I checked my running too: 11 min/km down to 5.1 for a 5k and 5.5 for a 10k. That faster pace is a literal new heart, pumping more blood per beat.

The whole thing

Five things. That's the entire playbook.

Fat leaves through your breath. To lose it you have to put in less carbon than you breathe out. That is a calorie deficit.

You can't out-train the plate. Fix food first. The workout helps, it does not excuse it.

Protein is the lever. Around 1.6g per kg. It costs more to digest, kills hunger, and saves your muscle.

Build the oxygen engine. Lift to keep muscle, Zone 2 to burn fat, and name the exact thing you are training.

Give it a number and a schedule. Then just repeat it. Discipline beats motivation every time.

It took me ten years to learn something I could have understood in an afternoon. The science is not the hard part. Starting is.
