The insighting hub.

One agent. Three research jobs. Zero research hires. Most teams treat customer research as a quarterly event: schedule interviews, wait three weeks, get a deck, forget it. This page shows the whole thing running daily instead, then walks you through building your own in a single working day. It is one piece of the machine behind a ₹1,000cr brand run by 5 to 10 people.

05 / Why it compounds

    
The loop is the product.

    
Building the hub takes a day. What makes it worth 15 researchers is the rhythm you run it on.

    

      
      
    
        
  • Run
    Same schedule, every time. Briefs weekly, listening daily. The agent earns trust through rhythm, the same way people do.
    • 
        
  • Read
    Read every finding, like an intern's first report. Never skim, never auto-trust a confidence score.
    • 
        
  • Correct
    Correct the prompt, not the finding. One edit to the master prompt kills that class of mistake forever. This is the compounding step.
    • 
        
  • Feed
    Let every decision read the dashboard. Ad scripts, product calls, launch briefs. Intelligence nobody uses is just a diary.
    • 
      

    

    

      The one rule people break
      
The dashboard is not a report you glance at once a quarter. It only works wired into the documents you already decide from. A dashboard that no workflow reads is a graveyard, not a system.

    

  







  

    
06 / Where these systems die

    
Four ways to kill it. Tap to see the fix.

    

      

        
Death no. 1
You farm the same 20 customers
tap for the fix →

        
The fix
Rotate through a large cohort and cap contact at once a month per person. The moment customers feel farmed, they stop telling you the truth, and the whole system runs on truth.

      

      

        
Death no. 2
The dashboard nobody opens
tap for the fix →

        
The fix
Never create a new destination. Point existing habits at it: the one line in claude.md, in your ad-script prompt, in your weekly planning doc. Rewire, don't add.

      

      

        
Death no. 3
Three jobs, three formats
tap for the fix →

        
The fix
One board, one five-field brief, one seven-column dashboard, for all three jobs. Every extra format multiplies agent errors and your correction workload.

      

      

        
Death no. 4
Nobody defines a good finding
tap for the fix →

        
The fix
"Customers seem unhappy" is not a finding. "62% cite no lock-in as the reason they bought" is. If you cannot tell whether the output did the job, you cannot manage the agent doing it.
