01 / The idea

One loop, five parts

In 2020, a team led by Tim Spector fed 1,100 people identical meals and published the results in Nature Medicine. Same food, completely different blood responses. Even identical twins diverged. Genes explained only 30% of it. If two people with the same DNA respond differently to the same meal, one product for every body is a broken promise. So the biggest health companies stopped betting on products and started building a loop around each customer.

04 / The gap

India still sells the product

India is the world's second largest wearables market by volume and one of the cheapest by value. The hardware is treated as a gadget, and the supplement shelf competes on ingredient labels. Ultrahuman, from Bangalore, is the one Indian company running the full playbook on this page. One, out of roughly 9,000 registered Indian healthtech startups.

My prediction

Standalone health products in India get squeezed to quick-commerce margins within three years. The profit moves to whoever owns the customer's loop. The next big Indian health company won't launch a product. It will launch a system that learns your body.
