First principles

Strip away the product language and the claim becomes easier to inspect.

1. Fuel

Being alive costs ATP.

Your heart, brain, liver, muscles, immune system, digestion, and repair machinery all spend energy even when you sit still.

2. Exhaust

A metabolic cart reads the smoke.

It estimates energy use from oxygen coming in and carbon dioxide going out. Think of it as an electricity meter for the body.

3. Proxy

Heart rate is only one wire.

Same heart rate can mean different energy cost depending on heat, caffeine, sleep debt, stress, fuel mix, and fatigue.

What is solid, early, and missing

Separate physiology from product proof. That is where most wearable debates go wrong.

Humans have a real metabolic cost.Energy demand changes with rest, exercise, food, stress, heat, sleep, and illness.
Solid science

A metabolic cart can estimate energy expenditure from oxygen and carbon dioxide exchange.

Heart rate is a weak energy proxy.It often tracks effort, but it misses fuel mix, efficiency, heat, and stress effects.
Solid science

Independent wearable validation shows heart rate accuracy is much better than calorie or energy expenditure accuracy.

Entropy tracked a metabolic cart at r = 0.93.Deepinder says this came from 100+ cardio sessions.
Promising claim

Strong if replicated. Still needs sample size, raw method, train-test split, error bands, and independent validation.

Lower minima means longer life.This is tempting, but too simple.
Context needed

Low resting cost can mean recovery. It can also mean low lean mass, under-eating, thyroid issues, illness, or frailty.
