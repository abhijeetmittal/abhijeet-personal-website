01 / What the list shows

Why these companies won

Most of them did not win because of a better product. The supplement, the pill or the workout was usually one you could already get somewhere else. They won by taking a health problem that is awkward or confusing to deal with and making it simple to buy and easy to repeat.

Four business models

Most of the large companies fall into one of four types: branded consumer-health portfolios, subscription telehealth, functional nutrition, and membership fitness. The rest are variations on these.

One problem, done well

Hair loss, weight, fertility, sleep, anxiety, a daily supplement habit. Each company picked one problem that people struggle to deal with and built a simple, repeatable product around it.

Funding is not revenue

A large funding round is enough to put a company on this list, but it does not prove the business works. Cards marked Funding still need a revenue check, so each card shows how its figure was sourced.

Big companies buy, not build

Look at the acquisitions. Unilever, HUL, Nestlé and others are not buying new science. They are buying customers and habits they could not build on their own.

The takeaway

Health and wellness looks like one big category, but it works like a trust business. The companies that scaled sold more than a supplement or a session. They sold a simple, repeatable way to handle one specific health worry, and a reason to keep paying for it.

03 / Method and caveats

How this was built

What is included: a company is on the list if a public source shows it above roughly USD 30m in revenue or USD 15m raised, in consumer health, personal care, nutrition, telehealth, weight, women's health, mental wellness, fitness, diagnostics, or wellness retail.

How the numbers are sourced: official filings first, then business press, then public databases. Funding-based and estimated figures are marked in amber on each card. A Funding figure means the company raised that much, not that it earns it, so treat those with more caution. All figures are from the most recent public year available and will change over time.
