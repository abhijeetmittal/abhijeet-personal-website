01 / What the map shows

The pattern is not "wellness." It is consumerized trust.

Pull back from the logos and the winners rhyme. Almost none of them won on a molecule or a workout. They won by taking an embarrassing, confusing, or high-friction health job and turning it into a calm, repeatable product system you could buy without thinking twice.

four models, not fifty

The public-market winners cluster into four shapes: branded consumer-health portfolios, subscription telehealth, functional nutrition, and membership-led fitness. Everything else is a variation on one of these.

the job, productized

Hair loss, weight, fertility, sleep, anxiety, a daily greens habit. Each winner picked one high-friction job and built a repeatable system around it, then earned the right to sell the next one.

funding is not scale

A big round clears the bar to appear here. It does not prove a business. Cards tagged Funding need a second-pass revenue check before you size them, which is why the source sits on every card.

distribution beats discovery

The acquisitions tell the real story: incumbents are not buying science, they are buying audiences and habits they could not build in-house. The asset being priced is trust, not IP.

the one line to keep

Health and wellness is not a category, it is a trust business wearing a category's clothes. The companies that scaled did not sell supplements or sessions. They sold a reason to stop worrying about one specific thing, on a schedule.

03 / Method & caveats

How this was built

Inclusion: a company appears if a public source puts it above roughly USD 30m in revenue or USD 15m raised, within consumer health, personal care, nutrition, telehealth, weight, women's health, mental wellness, fitness, diagnostics, or wellness retail.

Source standard: Tier 1 is official filings, Tier 2 is credible business press, Tier 3 is public databases. Funding-based and estimated figures are flagged in amber on each card. A Funding qualifier clears the threshold but does not prove commercial scale, so size those names with a second pass. Figures reflect the most recent public year available and will drift, so this is a snapshot, not a live ledger.

Highest-revenue anchors: Kenvue, Haleon, Amway, Reckitt Health, Nestlé Health Science, Unilever Health & Wellbeing, Herbalife, Life Time, Peloton, iHerb, Edgewell, BellRing, H&H Group, Nu Skin, Hims & Hers, Celsius, Planet Fitness, Progyny, USANA.
